About Me

I'm a third-year student studying a Bachelor's of Science degree in Computer Science and Philosophy with a Minor in Mathematics at Northeastern University Khoury College of Computer Sciences. My journey thus far has been all about mastering my technical ability in computer science to maximize my positive, social impact in careful engineering. My academic history has been a messy conglomeration of competitive mathematics, consuming literature on tech, law & media and tinkering around a myriad of projects. My experiences defined my ultimate interest: Technology, democracy and social change. You will find me reading, making or talking about all things related them. Some recent activities that have given me fulfillment is serving as Executive Vice President at the TAMID Group on campus, working as a Software Engineering Intern at UKG, and keeping up with my reading and personal projects.

My early education was founded on the principle that math was everything. Ever since I was young, I participated in competitive math teams, went to Idea Math Camp, and then much later, a six-week intensive summer studies at Hampshire College Summer Studies in Mathematics, where I really began to appreciate the quirks and scope of the field. I took my interests into computer science, where I enjoyed the marriage of theoretical thinking and practical application. In 2020, I was accepted into the MIT PRIMES's Computer Science Research track where I worked with Jules Drean, a graduate student at MIT CSAIL, on a project called Non-Fungible Objects (NFO): Hard-to-Counterfeit Virtual Assets Based On Trusted-Hardware. At Northeastern, I expanded my knowledge about computer science into the world of full stack web development and AI at the TAMID Group on campus and at Samsung AI. I soon became obsessed with computer science as it became the medium for both my quantitative and creative expression. During the same year, I read Superintelligence: Risks, Dangers, Strategies by Nick Bostrom and became enraptured by tech, ethics, and society. My activities this summer reflected all my interests: I strengthened my technical literacy at a Software Engineering Internship at Ampion, PBC and worked with Professor Patterson at the Northeastern University to work on his code synthesizer research project combating automation bias, and bringing AI into programming languages.
