ABOUT ME

I'm a third-year student studying a Bachelor's of Science degree in Computer Science and Philosophy with a Minor in Mathematics at Northeastern University Khoury College of Computer Sciences. My journey so far have shaped my core passion: building technology that guides and empowers people to intuitively make better decisions and live good lives. Right now, I conduct research in fostering virtues online, nudging techniques, and human-centric design at the Plural Connections Group. Outside of my life in tech, I've enjoy teaching underclassmen Algorithms & Data Structures as the Director of Tech Consulting at TAMID, taking too long to finish the Dune series, and spending time in the great outdoors via hiking and snowboarding.

My early education was founded on the principle that math was everything. Ever since I was young, I participated (poorly) in competitive math teams, went to Idea Math Camp, and then much later, a six-week intensive summer studies at Hampshire College Summer Studies in Mathematics, where I really began to appreciate the quirks and scope of the field. I took my interests into computer science, where I enjoyed the marriage of theoretical thinking and practical application. In 2020, I was accepted into the MIT PRIMES's Computer Science Research track where I worked with Jules Drean, a graduate student at MIT CSAIL, on a project called Non-Fungible Objects (NFO): Hard-to-Counterfeit Virtual Assets Based On Trusted-Hardware. I joined Northeastern in 2022 where I expanded my knowledge about computer science into the world of full stack web development and AI at the TAMID Group on campus and at Samsung AI. I soon became obsessed with computer science as it became the medium for both my quantitative and creative expression. During the same year, I read Superintelligence: Risks, Dangers, Strategies by Nick Bostrom and became enraptured by tech, ethics, and society. My activities during 2023 reflected all my interests: I strengthened my technical literacy at a Software Engineering Internship at Ampion, PBC and worked with Professor Patterson at the Northeastern University to work on his code synthesizer research project combating automation bias, and bringing AI into programming languages. In 2024, I started working at the Plural Connections Group as a Research Engineer Co-op on their Intellectual Humility initiative and led TAMID as their Executive Vice President. All in all, my work isn’t just about writing code—it’s about exploring how technology shapes the way we think, interact, and solve problems. And that’s what keeps me excited for what’s next.
